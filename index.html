Total reflection for electromagnetic waves including lights is a common phenomenon for the incidence wave from one medium going into another medium. When the refractive index of the incidence medium is larger than the refractive index of the transmitted medium, total reflection starts to occur as incidence angle growing to a specific value. Before talking about the details, let's review the relationship between incidence angle and refracted angle, which is the Snell's refraction law:


The total reflection originates when the refraction angle forms a 90 degree and thus disappears in the refracted medium. In such cases, we can only observe the reflected wave in the incidence medium. And at all angles less than the critical angle, both refraction and reflection occur in varying proportions.
